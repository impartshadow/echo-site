## Root-cause chain

1. Surface: harness scaffolding ('[Channel: ...]', '[System: Bot just restarted...]') repeatedly stripped from outbound #shadow-hq messages, some reduced to empty strings.
2. The restart flow injects a system preamble into the model's context, and the model echoes it verbatim into its response text instead of treating it as metadata.
3. The identical 4-message leak cluster recurs three times, meaning the bot restarted at least three times in the window and the echo happens deterministically on every restart — the leak is structural, not stochastic.
4. Concurrently, restarts sever the session's grounding state: post-restart turns assert facts and revenue figures from stale memory (state-assertion-grounding warn, 2x partial-evidence-flag blocks) because the verification habit lives in conversation context that the restart destroyed.
5. Structural cause: the restart/resume pipeline passes harness-internal framing through the same channel as user-facing content and provides no re-grounding step, so every restart simultaneously leaks scaffolding downstream and produces ungrounded assertions upstream — the guards catch the symptoms at egress but nothing repairs the restart boundary itself.

## The contract that would have caught it

**`restart-boundary-regrounding-gate`**

- **Trigger:** First outbound message composed after a bot restart is detected (restart preamble present in context, or state/bot_restart_log.jsonl entry newer than session start).
- **Precondition:** The restart preamble has been consumed as metadata (never present in the outbound text buffer), AND at least one ground-truth read (session_handoff.md, relevant state/ file, or git log) has executed this turn before any definitive claim is emitted.
- **Why it catches this:** Every event in this window traces to a restart boundary: the 12 scaffold leaks are the preamble echoed post-restart, and the ungrounded assertion/revenue fires are post-restart turns answering from memory the restart invalidated. A pre-check at the boundary would strip the preamble before generation (eliminating all FM-005 fires at the source instead of sanitizing them at egress) and force the same-turn read that would have prevented the FM-014/FM-026 fires.

## Why this matters

Prompt-only guardrails would have posted raw '[System: Bot just restarted]' scaffolding to a user-facing channel twelve times and shipped fabricated revenue figures twice — the model regenerated the exact same violations across three restart cycles, proving instructions don't survive context resets. Deterministic egress contracts caught every instance with zero user-visible damage: matched tokens stripped, ungrounded $-claims replaced with '$0 confirmed'. The trace also shows the honest limit of enforcement — guards contain symptoms while the violation telemetry itself pinpoints the structural fix (the restart boundary), which is the diagnostic loop prompt-engineering can't provide.
